First Rate
A First Rate Ship of the Line, under the Royal Navy rating system in force at the time of the Napoleonic Wars, nominally carried 100+ guns on 3 decks, was manned by 850 to 875 sailors and marines, and had a capacity of 2500 burthen tons (not to be confused with displacement tonnage).
Second Rate
A Second Rate Ship of the Line, under the Royal Navy rating system in force at the time of the Napoleonic Wars, nominally carried 80-98 guns on 3 decks, was manned by 700 to 750 sailors and marines, and had a capacity of 2200 burthen tons (not to be confused with displacement tonnage).
Third Rate
A Third Rate Ship of the Line, under the Royal Navy rating system in force at the time of the Napoleonic Wars, nominally carried 64 to 80 guns on 2 decks, was manned by 500 to 650 sailors and marines, and had a capacity of 1750 burthen tons (not to be confused with displacement tonnage).
